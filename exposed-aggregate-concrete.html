Timing the wash correctly

We know the window for each mix in Queensland conditions and we are set up and ready before the truck leaves. The reveal is consistent from one end to the other.

Even, consistent stone depth

Patchy aggregate — too deep in some spots, barely exposed in others — is the most common failure. We float to a consistent depth so the wash-back is uniform across the full area.

Multiple seal coats

We acid-wash the surface after the reveal, then build up seal coats to the wet-look finish. Shortcuts on sealing leave the stone dull and unprotected within a season.

Pour & place

Common questions

Questions we get asked before every job.

What is exposed aggregate and how is it different from plain concrete?

Exposed aggregate has the top layer of cement paste washed away after the pour to reveal the decorative stone underneath. The stone is then sealed to a wet-look finish that gives the surface colour, depth and natural grip. Plain concrete stays as-poured — the stone aggregate is hidden inside the slab.

What stone ranges do you use?

We pour both the Boral Exposé range and the Nielsens range. Boral offers consistent fine aggregates with strong colour options — salt and pepper is the most popular for modern Caboolture and Morayfield homes. Nielsens use locally sourced stone with more natural variation in size and tone.

Is exposed aggregate slippery when wet?

No — the exposed stone gives natural texture underfoot, which is why it is popular around pools, on sloped driveways and on paths. It provides better wet-weather grip than a smooth or power-floated concrete surface. We can advise on stone size and depth of reveal to match your slip-resistance requirements.

How often does exposed aggregate need resealing?

For a driveway, we typically recommend a reseal every 2–3 years to maintain the wet-look finish and protect the stone against staining. Pool surrounds and paths, which see less traffic and UV exposure, usually go longer. Resealing is a quick job and keeps the surface looking the way it did when it was poured.

How long does it take and when can I use it?

Most residential exposed aggregate pours are done in a day. Light foot traffic after 24 hours; we recommend 7 days before vehicles and 28 days for full strength. The seal coats are applied over two visits in the days after the pour, which is when the final wet-look finish appears.
