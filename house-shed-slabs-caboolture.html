Slab quote questions

Questions we get asked before every job.

What does a shed slab or house pad cost in Caboolture?

Pricing depends on size, thickness, reinforcement requirements, finish type and site conditions. A basic shed slab is priced differently from a house pad or workshop floor under heavy load. We give a fixed written price after measuring up — not a loose per-square-metre estimate that changes at handover. Contact us for a same-day quote response.

How thick should a shed slab be?

A standard residential shed slab is 100mm, but the right thickness depends on what is going on it. Heavy equipment, vehicles or a workshop floor may need 125mm or more. We confirm thickness, reo size and concrete mix design before quoting so the slab is built for the actual load, not just the minimum.

Do you include reinforcement and drainage?

Yes. All our slabs include steel reinforcement sized to the load requirements — mesh or reo bar placed on chairs with correct cover. We also assess falls, thresholds and drainage before the pour starts so water moves away from the slab and structure, not under it.

How long before we can load the slab?

Light foot traffic is fine after 24–48 hours. We recommend keeping vehicles and equipment off for 7 days and the slab reaches full design strength at 28 days. You get a written handover sheet with the exact curing schedule for your pour — no guesswork.

Do you service nearby suburbs?

Yes. We regularly work across Caboolture, Morayfield, Caboolture South, Bellmere, Upper Caboolture, Burpengary and nearby Moreton Bay suburbs. Contact us with your suburb and we will confirm availability.
